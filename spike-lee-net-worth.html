Spike Lee’s Early Career

Spike Lee was born Shelton Jackson Lee on March 20, 1957, in Atlanta, Georgia.

He grew up in a middle-class Brooklyn neighborhood, where he developed a passion for film and photography.

He attended Morehouse College in Atlanta, where he majored in mass communication and made his first student film, Last Hustle in Brooklyn.

Later, he then enrolled at New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts, where he earned his Master of Fine Arts degree in film and television in 1978.

His thesis film, Joe’s Bed-Stuy Barbershop: We Cut Heads, won the Student Academy Award for Best Film.

Lee made his feature film debut with She’s Gotta Have It in 1986.

The low-budget comedy about a young woman and her three lovers was shot in two weeks for $175,000.

It grossed over $7 million at the box office and received critical acclaim.

It also introduced Lee’s trademark style of addressing controversial social issues with humor and insight.

Lee also played the role of Mars Blackmon, a character he later reprised for a series of Nike commercials featuring Michael Jordan.

Lee followed up his success with School Daze in 1988, a musical comedy-drama about life at a historically black college.

The film featured an ensemble cast of young actors, many of whom would become regular collaborators with Lee in his future projects.

The film also tackled themes such as racism, classism, sexism, and colorism within the black community.

Lee’s next film, Do the Right Thing in 1989, was his breakthrough masterpiece.

The film depicted the racial tensions and violence that erupted on the hottest day of the year in a Brooklyn neighborhood.

The film was praised for its powerful and provocative portrayal of the complex realities of race relations in America.

It also earned Lee his first Academy Award nomination for Best Original Screenplay.

The film holds a prominent place as one of the greatest of all time, earning selection for preservation in the National Film Registry by the Library of Congress.

Spike Lee’s Filmography and Achievements

Spike Lee continued to make films that explored various aspects of the African-American experience and culture throughout the 1990s and 2000s.

  • Red Hook Summer (2012), is a coming-of-age drama about a boy who spends the summer with his religious grandfather in Brooklyn.
  • Oldboy (2013), is a remake of the Korean thriller about a man (Josh Brolin) who seeks revenge after being imprisoned for 20 years without explanation.

  • Da 5 Bloods (2020), is a war drama about four black veterans who return to Vietnam to find the remains of their fallen leader (Chadwick Boseman) and a buried treasure.
